# Learner Guide — Hanzi Graded Reader

Welcome! This app makes short Chinese stories **just for you**. Every story is built from
characters you already know, plus a few new ones to learn. You don't need to know every
character before you start — if you get stuck, just **tap the character** and the app shows you
how to say it, what it means, and how to write it. The more you read (and finish your stories),
the more characters the app teaches you next.

---

## 1. Create your profile (onboarding)

From the home page, click **New learner**. You'll fill out a short form:

- **Name** — whatever you'd like to be called.
- **Reading companion** — pick a friendly character who shows up across your stories. It's just
  for fun and flavor.
- **Favorite genre** — pick a vibe (adventure, mystery, sci-fi, fantasy, history, friendship,
  sport, slice of life), or **🎲 surprise me** to let the app choose each time. You can change
  this for any individual story later.
- **Placement** — this is how the app figures out which characters you already know. Pick **one**:
  - **Declare an HSK level (1–6)** — fastest if you've studied HSK. Pick your level and the app
    assumes you know the characters up to it.
  - **Paste characters I know** — paste a vocab list or any Chinese passage. The app keeps the
    distinct characters in it as your "known" set.
  - **Pick from a character grid** — characters appear most-common-first. **Tap** the ones you
    know. Flip on **"Mark down to here"** and tap a character to mark *it and everything more
    common* as known in one go, then tap individual tiles to fine-tune. The grid is paged
    (100 per page) — use **Previous / Next**.
  - **Start from zero (bootstrap)** — for true beginners. The app starts you at the very
    beginning with extra help (pinyin is shown by default).

Click **Create learner** and you're in.

> Note: there's no settings screen yet, so your companion and favorite genre are chosen here at
> onboarding. (You can still override the genre per story.)

---

## 2. Your home page

Your home page (`/learners/[your-id]`) is your hub:

- The header shows your placement method, companion, and genre.
- **Progress** (top right) opens your stats screen. **All learners** goes back to the start.
- **New story** form — generate a fresh story (next section).
- **Story library / seeds** — pick a famous story to have retold in your vocabulary.
- **Stories** — every story you've made. Each card shows the new characters it teaches
  (*targets*), how much of it is made of characters you know (*coverage %*), and a **branch** tag
  if it continues from an earlier story.

Click any story card to open it.

---

## 3. Generate a story

In the **New story** form:

1. Either **tap a genre chip** *or* **type your own theme** in the box (for example,
   *"a dragon who can't fly"*). These are alternatives — picking a chip clears the text box, and
   typing clears the chip.
2. Click **Generate**. It takes a few seconds (you'll see *"Writing your story…"*).

Sometimes generation **fails** with a short error message — that's normal, it just means the app
couldn't keep the story easy enough on that try. Just press **Generate** again.

You can also use the **seed library** to retell a well-known story (folktales, history, classics)
using only the words you know.

---

## 4. Read a story

Opening a story takes you to the reader. This is where you spend your time:

- **Big characters.** The **Pinyin** toggle (top right) is **off by default** — try reading
  without it first. (If you started from zero, pinyin is on to help you.)
- **Tap any character** to open a panel showing its **pinyin**, **meaning**, the smaller
  **components** it's built from, and a **stroke-order animation** you can **Replay**. This is
  your safety net — tap freely whenever you're unsure.
- **Comprehension questions** — answer the multiple-choice questions to check you understood.
- **Choices** — at the end you can pick how the story continues. Your pick creates a new
  **branch** story that keeps the same characters and setting.
- **"I'm done reading"** — **press this when you finish.** It tells the app you've read the
  story so it can update which characters you're due to review and choose what to teach you next.
  If you skip it, your next stories won't adapt as well.

You don't have to track anything yourself — the app quietly notices which characters you lingered
on or tapped to reveal, and uses that to schedule reviews automatically.

---

## 5. Track your progress

The **Progress** screen is your goal board:

- A big **"characters you can now read"** number.
- A **curriculum bar** showing how far through the character order you are, with counts of
  characters you're *learning*, *reviewing*, and have *mastered*. Mastered grows as you read and
  answer questions correctly over time.
- **Coming up next** — a preview of the characters your upcoming stories will introduce.
- **Reward texts** — real poems and famous lines. Each one **unlocks** once you can read about
  **95%** of its characters. Watch the bars fill up as you learn.

---

## 6. Tips for getting the most out of it

- **Leave pinyin off** and try to read first — only toggle it or tap a character when you're
  genuinely stuck. That's how the reading actually sticks.
- **Always tap "I'm done reading"** at the end. It's what keeps your stories getting better.
- **Mix it up** — different genres, your own themes, and the seed library all keep stories
  fresh while teaching the same characters.
- Reading a little every day beats one long session — the review schedule works best with
  steady practice.
